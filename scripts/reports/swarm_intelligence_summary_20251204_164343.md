## Idea 100 (Agent 24, deepseek-reasoner)

## Swarm Intelligence Opportunity
Evolutionary competition swarm where 1000 agents compete in strategy tournaments, with the fittest strategies reproducing and mutating while unfit strategies die off, creating emergent trading strategies through evolutionary pressure.

## The Core Insight
A single agent can optimize parameters within a predefined strategy, but evolutionary competition discovers entirely new strategy architectures that no human designer would conceive. The parallelism enables simultaneous exploration of the vast combinatorial space of trading rules, technical indicators, and position-sizing algorithms, while competition provides the selection pressure that drives the system toward profitability. What emerges is not just better parameters, but novel strategy genotypes - unique combinations of market signals and execution logic that exploit subtle, non-obvious market inefficiencies.

## Concrete Implementation
1000 agents divided into 100 tribes of 10 agents each. Each tribe specializes in a different market regime (volatility regimes, trend regimes, mean-reversion regimes). Every trading day:
1. **Tournament Phase**: All agents trade the same capital allocation (simulated)
2. **Fitness Evaluation**: Agents ranked by risk-adjusted returns (Sharpe + Sortino + Max DD)
3. **Reproduction**: Top 20% "breed" - their strategy genomes combine via crossover
4. **Mutation**: 5% random mutation rate introduces novel trading rules
5. **Extinction**: Bottom 30% die off, freeing resources for new agents
6. **Migration**: Best agents move between tribes to share innovations

Each agent's "genome" encodes: signal combinations (3-7 indicators), position-sizing logic, stop-loss rules, time horizons, and asset preference weights. The system runs continuously, with daily evolution cycles.

## Expected Edge
30-50% annual alpha through discovery of "black swan resistant" strategies that:
1. **Evolve regime-adaptation**: Agents develop innate ability to detect and adapt to changing market conditions
2. **Discover nonlinear signal interactions**: Find synergistic combinations of 4+ indicators that individually show no edge
3. **Optimize for unseen market states**: Through genetic diversity, the population maintains strategies optimized for market conditions that haven't occurred yet
4. **Emergent risk management**: The evolutionary process naturally selects for strategies with positive skew and low correlation to market beta

The mechanism is ecological - as agents compete for limited capital, they evolve toward strategies that exploit ever-more-subtle inefficiencies, creating a "predator-prey" dynamic where successful strategies eventually become crowded, driving further evolution.

## Why This Is Non-Obvious
Evolutionary systems produce solutions that are counterintuitive to human designers. A 1000-agent swarm could discover:
- **Paradoxical strategies**: Buy when volatility spikes AND momentum is negative (contradicts conventional wisdom)
- **Multi-timeframe arbitrage**: Exploit millisecond vs. daily signal mispricings simultaneously
- **Self-correcting bias**: The population maintains contradictory strategies that hedge each other
- **Emergent market-making**: Agents might evolve to provide liquidity during specific volatility regimes

The key insight is that evolution discovers "local maxima" in the strategy landscape that are invisible to gradient-based optimization. While everyone else is tuning parameters, the swarm is discovering entirely new dimensions of the problem space. The cost structure ($0.05 per agent) makes this feasible at scale for the first time, enabling evolutionary search across a space of 10^100+ possible strategy combinations.
